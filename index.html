About

I am a fifth-year PhD candidate in the Department of Mathematics at the University of California, Riverside, advised by Dr. Brian Collier. I received my M.S. from UC Riverside and my B.S. from Biola University.

My research interests lie in differential and complex geometry, with a particular focus on Higgs bundles, flat connections, and the geometry of moduli spaces. I am also drawn to Teichmüller theory and its deep connections to these structures.

Alongside research, I am committed to mathematics education. I have taught courses from precalculus through multivariable calculus and topology at UC Riverside and California Baptist University, both as instructor of record and teaching assistant.

This fall, I will be joining the faculty at Oklahoma Baptist University in Shawnee, Oklahoma. I look forward to continuing my research and building a strong teaching program there.
